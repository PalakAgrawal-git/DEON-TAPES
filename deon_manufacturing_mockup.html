Manufacturing & technology

How Deon builds an adhesive tape

Every Deon tape is three decisions made together — the adhesive system, the backing it is coated onto, and the conversion that turns master rolls into finished slit rolls, log rolls and spools. Controlling all three in-house lets us tune peel, shear, tack and temperature to your application, not to a catalogue average.

The sections below walk the full chain: from adhesive chemistry and substrate selection, through in-house PVC calendering, coating, curing and converting, into the lab that qualifies every batch, and out to the development, R&D and compliance programmes that keep that output certified and repeatable.

Adhesive technologies

Adhesive chemistry, matched to the joint

The adhesive decides whether a tape holds. Deon formulates rubber, synthetic-rubber hot-melt and acrylic (solvent and water-based) systems and prepares silicone systems in-house, then coats each onto the backing the duty calls for.

Selection follows the surface and the duty cycle: rubber gives aggressive instant tack for insulation and harness work; acrylics trade some initial grab for UV, ageing and chemical resistance and high-temperature shear; silicone holds where the others cannot. Coat weight, cross-link density and tackifier loading are then tuned to the substrate.

Rubber & hot-melt systems

Natural and synthetic rubber and synthetic hot-melt (SBC) adhesives for high initial tack and hand-tearable PVC insulation and harness tapes. Hot-melt grades run clean at high line speeds with reliable cold-temperature adhesion.

Acrylic (solvent & water-based)

Solvent and emulsion acrylic PSA chemistries for UV, plasticiser and ageing resistance with long-term shear from −40 to +150 °C — tuned for PET, glass, coated metal and medium-energy plastics.

How we use it →

Silicone systems

Silicone / polysiloxane adhesives that hold to 200 °C and above and bond to polyimide and low-surface-energy substrates — specified for high-temperature masking through reflow, wave-solder and powder-coat cure.

Backing materials

The backing carries the performance

The backing sets a tape's mechanical, thermal, dielectric and dimensional behaviour. Deon coats onto a broad library — soft PVC, polyester (PET) and polyimide film, woven glass cloth, PE foam, aluminium foil, HDPE fabric, BOPP, and saturated tissue and crepe papers.

Backing choice governs conformability, tensile and tear strength, temperature class and breakdown voltage. Matching the right backing to the adhesive is the core of every Deon construction.

Soft PVC film

Our own calendered soft PVC — flexible, flame-retardant backing for electrical insulation (31700), wire harness (50W13), floor marking (41860) and pipe-wrapping tapes.

How we use it →

PET & polyimide film

Thin, dimensionally stable polyester and polyimide films for Class B–H insulation, coil and capacitor winding, splicing and fine masking — coated and slit to tight tolerances.

How we use it →

Glass cloth & PE foam

Woven glass-fabric backings for high-temperature insulation to 300 °C, and closed-cell PE foam carriers for gasketing, cushioning and stress-relieving mounting.

Foil, fabric & reinforced

Aluminium foil for sealing and HVAC, HDPE fabric and BOPP for packaging, and mono- / cross-filament reinforcement for high-tensile bundling and unitising.

Soft PVC film

Deon calenders its own soft PVC film

Beyond tapes, Deon manufactures its own soft PVC film on in-house calendering lines. Producing the film — not just buying it — gives control over thickness, hardness, colour and finish, and keeps quality and supply consistent from compound to finished roll.

The same film feeds two outputs: it is coated and converted into Deon's own PVC tapes, and supplied as stand-alone soft PVC film for lamination, converting and industrial use — a level of vertical integration most tape suppliers do not have.

Manufacturing capabilities

From coating line to finished roll

Deon runs dedicated solvent, hot-melt and water-based (emulsion) coating lines, so each chemistry is coated on its optimal process, with coat weight held to tight tolerances across the web and in-line drying and curing.

Master rolls then move into converting — high-speed slitting and rewinding to your widths, lengths, cores and put-ups, including printed cores and private-label. One facility, one chain of quality control, shorter lead times.

Testing & validation

An in-house lab that qualifies every batch

Performance claims are only as good as the test behind them. Deon's laboratory characterises every construction to ASTM D1000 and recognised methods — peel adhesion to steel and to backing, tensile and elongation, breakdown voltage and dielectric strength, and temperature resistance across the service range.

Results feed both the technical data sheet and incoming-to-outgoing batch QC, so what ships matches what was specified.

Custom product development

Developed to specification

When a stock construction does not fit, Deon develops one. We start from the joint — the substrates, temperatures, dwell and cure window, geometry and volume — and work back to an adhesive / backing / coat-weight that meets it. Thickness, width, length and colour are all customisable.

Candidates are sampled, tested against defined acceptance criteria and iterated before any tooling is committed, then scaled with documented parameters into converting, OEM and private-label supply.

R&D & innovation

Where the next Deon construction comes from

Deon's R&D tracks where industrial assembly is heading — bonded assembly replacing rivets and welds, thermal management in denser electronics and EV battery packs, reliable bonding to low-surface-energy plastics and composites, and thinner dielectric films that survive higher reflow temperatures.

Work spans new adhesive chemistries and tackifier systems, functional fillers, advanced backing and liner combinations, and coating-process scale-up — each programme validated through the same lab that releases production.

Sustainability & compliance

Certified, documented and accountable

OEM and converter customers cannot specify what they cannot certify. Deon runs documented ISO 9001:2015 quality, ISO 14001:2015 environmental and ISO 45001:2018 health & safety systems, with IATF 16949:2016 for automotive — alongside UL 510, IS 7809, REACH and RoHS declarations. Data sheets, declarations of conformity and batch traceability support qualification.

On sustainability, priorities are practical: water-based and solvent-free adhesive systems that cut VOC emissions, optimised coat weights that reduce material use, and converting that minimises waste — measurable improvement that does not compromise the bond.
